Chair Prototyping

For this in class project, I worked with my fellow classmates to build a chair for a customer. Our “customer” was an astronaut from the “Simpsons” who wanted to be able to sit without floating around while drinking his Tang. Our design was a memory foam inverse chair which would cushion him from the top. It also had a lever so the seat portion could raise and lower him into the cushion on the top so he could be secure and not float around. Then we made prototypes out of hard materials such as popsicle sticks. This was difficult as they were long and hard to form. We made a second prototype out of soft materials such as string and pipe-cleaners. These were much easier to fold but harder to hold structure. Lastly, we made a life size prototype out of cardboard and pipe cleaners. We used the wall for support and the chair provided to make modifications. Overall this was a fun experience talking through ideas, classifying them based on reasonability and importance, then building makeshift models.
